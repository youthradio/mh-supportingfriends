Scenario 1

Teen: “Hey mom, why’d you go through my phone? I told you nothing happened last night. If you had just asked, I would have let you look through it.”
YELLING “I had to! You seemed upset when you came home.”
calmly “I’m sorry. I was just worried about you. Next time I’ll talk to you first.”

Scenario 2

Your teen wants to go out to the movies with friends for a midnight showing of their favorite series. You say…
“I’m sorry, I'll have to put my foot down and say no. It’s just not safe enough in the city at night to be going to a movie.”
“If you create a plan for how you’re gonna get there and the precautions you’ll take to stay safe and in touch with me, then we can continue this conversation.”

Scenario 3

Teen: “I feel like you don’t pay enough attention to me. It’s beginning to affect me because I feel neglected.”
“You don’t ever come to me, so how would I know? Don’t you ever talk to me in that tone.”
“I didn’t realize that was how you felt. How could we work on this together?”

Scenario 4

Teen: “I’m really stressed out about my upcoming test in calculus. I haven’t been doing so well in this class and I don’t know what to do.”
“You just need to study and try harder, that’s all.”
“Is there a specific part that you are struggling with? Is there anything I could do to help you out?”

Scenario 5

Teen: “Sometimes, it’s like I’m in my own world because it feels like no one is listening or no one cares to even understand me.”
“What do you mean you’re in your own world? There are so many people around you! Are you criticizing how I raised you? I haven’t done anything wrong. I always listen to you! This conversation is OVER. Go to your room.”
“Thank you for letting me know how you feel. Let’s talk about it. I want to hear you out.”

Scenario 6

On your only off day of the month, your teen comes up to you and asks if you want to hang out with them. Teen : “Hey Mom, I saw this flyer about the Art and Soul festival and it seems really cool! Do you think we could go?”
“I’m sorry. Those kinds of scenes are too crowded and noisy for me, and I’m not that interested in it anyway. I need to lay low this weekend. Mommy is just too tired.”
“Okay, we can go! Maybe for just a couple of hours? And then we can go somewhere less crowded, like grab ice cream?”

Scenario 7

Your teen is talking about their class schedule for the upcoming school year. They’re really confused about why they need to take trigonometry. They think that schools should just get rid of math all together and they should get to choose what they want to learn.
You interrupt them midway through their explanation and tell them: “The choice shouldn’t be yours because a lot of kids would not be ready to make that decision. It would be chaos!”
You listen to explanation all the way through and relate to their feelings — maybe saying, “I hated math, too!” or “That’s how I felt about French.” And then you go on: “But structure is necessary. It’s good to be exposed to everything because you won’t know what you like until you try it.”
